The AI Confidentiality Kit

The exact prompt and checklist for using AI on client matters — so a client's identity never reaches the model in the first place.

1 The AI Confidentiality Prompt

Paste this at the top of any chat before you share matter facts. It tells the AI to work only with placeholders and never ask for or infer real identities.

You are assisting a lawyer who must protect client confidentiality and privilege.

Ground rules for this entire conversation:
1. I will give you facts with identities already replaced by placeholders such as
   [CLIENT_1], [PARTY_2], [ACCOUNT_1], [DATE_1]. Treat each placeholder as a stable,
   opaque token.
2. Never ask me to reveal a real name, address, account number, SSN, date of birth,
   or any other identifier. If a real identity would help, ask me to add a new
   placeholder instead.
3. Do not guess, infer, or "fill in" who a placeholder might refer to. Do not invent
   identifying details.
4. Keep every placeholder exactly as written in your output so I can map it back
   locally. Do not rename, merge, or renumber them.
5. If I accidentally paste a real identifier, flag it and ask me to replace it with a
   placeholder before you continue.

Confirm you understand, then wait for the matter facts.

2 The AI Confidentiality Checklist

Run this before, during, and after any AI session that touches a client matter.

  • Redact before you paste. Replace every name, address, account/SSN, case number, and date with a placeholder — locally, before the text leaves your machine.
  • Strip indirect identifiers too. A unique fact pattern can identify a client even with names removed. Generalize specifics that aren't needed for the analysis.
  • Lead with the prompt above so the model commits to placeholder-only handling.
  • Check the AI's vendor terms. Confirm your inputs aren't used for training or retained beyond your control; use a no-training / business tier where required.
  • Confirm client consent & engagement terms permit using AI tools on the matter.
  • Review every output before relying on it — automated redaction over-flags and can also miss; you remain responsible.
  • Restore locally, not in the cloud. Map placeholders back to real values on your own device, never by sending the key to a third party.
This kit is practical guidance, not legal advice. You remain responsible for protecting client confidentiality under your jurisdiction's rules (e.g., ABA Model Rule 1.6).
